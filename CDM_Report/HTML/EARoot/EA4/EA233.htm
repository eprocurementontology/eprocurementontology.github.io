Combination of several lots to conduct comparative assessment of the tenders.<br/><br/>Additional Information:<br/><br/>The comparative assessment may refer to the selection criteria, award and value that apply to several lots.<br/><br/>Pending of discussion with eForms<br/><br/><br/><br/>Member States may provide that, where more than one lot may be awarded to the same tenderer, contracting authorities may award contracts combining several or all lots where they have specified in the contract notice or in the invitation to confirm interest that they reserve the possibility of doing so and indicate the lots or groups of lots that may be combined.<br/>

Information about the maximum value which can be spent, in a framework agreement, within a group of lots. This information can be provided when the maximum value of a group of lots is lower than the sum of values of individual lots (e.g. when the same budget is shared for several lots).<br/><br/><br/>This is used in the Contract Award Notice and Direct Award Pre-information (DAP, according to the eForms header).<br/><br/><br/>
