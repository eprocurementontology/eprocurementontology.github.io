A class of agent performing an activity in a specific context.<br/><font color="#333333"><br/></font>Additional information<br/><br/>In ePO, the roles are defined as classes identifying types of agents playing specific procurement parts. Thus the role of Buyers is inherent to Public Organisations, the role of Economic Operators, Subcontractos and Service Providers are inherent to businesses. ePO roles are concepts soundly established and frequently used in the domain of public procurement, such as Buyer, Economic Operator, Winner, Tenderer, Subcontractor and Service Provider, for example. <br/><br/>In the context of public procurement, a role may involve different responsibilities or ‘functions’ related to procurement in general or more specifically to the management or execution of specific procurement procedure. Examples of functions of procurement-related roles are: a Buyer with the function (responsibility) of providing access to the procurement documents, a CPB that has the responsibility of managing the budget of one or more buyers.<br/><br/>Nonetheless, public procurements may involve functions that are not directly related to the procurement activities nor procurement-specific roles. Thus, in public procurement there may be need of identifying organisations that are responsible for providing information on taxes in a specific region, <br/><br/>Technical note: roles can be seen as ‘metaclasses, which explains why, in ePO, roles are defined as “classes of agents”. As a metaclass, the role is a surrogate (a substitute of) of Agent. The lifecycle of an instance of role is totally dependent of the existence of an instance of Agent playing that role (role a ‘composition’ of Agent). <br/><br/>Check with Giorgia Lodi: Could we have triples with the class role as the object (punning)….I can see it for classes with no properties (e.g. Mediator, Reviewer, etc., which is one more reason for having them in the codelist), but not for Buyer, EO, Subcontractor, etc.<br/><br/>See this site: <a href="file://https://www.difference.wiki/role-vs-function/"><font color="#0563c1"><u>https://www.difference.wiki/role-vs-function/</u></font></a><br/><br/>
