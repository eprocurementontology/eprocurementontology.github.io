<li>In any Procurement Procedure, there is at least one Procuring Entity;</li><li>Procuring Entities are “Organizations”, appropriately identified and described (IDs, Names, Addresses, Contact Points, etc.);</li><li>Depending on its nature and main activity a Procuring Entity may be identified simply as a Contracting Authority (general procurement) or as a Contracting Entity pursuing the procurement of gas and heat, electricity, water, transport services, ports and airports, postal services and extraction of oil and gas and exploration for, or extraction of, coal or other solid fuels. A Contracting Entity may in turn be a Contracting Authority, a Public Undertaking or entities with special or exclusive rights (Procuring Entity Type code list);</li><li>For some Procurement Procedures, a Procuring Entity can join other Procuring Entities (Joint Procurement)</li><li>In these cases, the Procuring Entities participating in the Joint Procurement adopt one role (Procuring Entity Role Type code list), e.g. the lead of the group.</li><li>Procuring Entities are in general responsible for the both the management of the procurement procedure and the purchase.  However in some cases procuring entities may buy on behalf of other procuring entities or through other procuring entities ("Procuring Entity Role Type").</li></ol>
